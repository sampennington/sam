# How I Led Localisation at VEED

When I was at **VEED**, leadership announced that we'd be **localising the entire platform** the following quarter. It was an exciting initiative ‚Äî but also a chaotic one. There wasn't a clear plan, no one really owned it, and most teams already had jam-packed roadmaps.

Rather than let it fizzle out (and stress everyone out in the process), I decided to step up and bring some structure to it. I proposed that one of my teams lead the localisation effort ‚Äî creating a clear point of contact, setting direction, and actually getting it shipped.

---

## The Challenges

This wasn‚Äôt a super-technical project ‚Äî at least not at first glance.  
We just needed to serve a different set of copy depending on language, right?

The tricky parts turned out to be:
- **Communication** ‚Äì The project touched 8+ codebases and 10+ teams. Everyone needed to stay in sync and keep their own roadmaps aligned.  
- **Maintaining translation quality** ‚Äì Automated translations can get weird fast.  
- **Catching missing translations** ‚Äì Avoiding the dreaded `{{hero.button.copy}}` showing up in production.  
- **Measuring impact** ‚Äì Making sure all this effort actually moved the needle.

---

## Execution

We started from scratch: choosing tools, setting up infrastructure, and building the workflows.  
We landed on **Lokalise**, **i18next**, and a few **custom tools** we built along the way.

Everything was **feature-flagged**, since we couldn‚Äôt launch localisation half-done.

### Building the First Custom Tool

Pretty quickly, we hit our first big issue: **untranslated keys slipping through**.  
Occasionally, users would see things like `{{hero.button.copy}}` ‚Äî not ideal.

We knew this would keep happening as more teams joined in, so we built our first internal tool:

- Validated that every key in our translation JSON files actually existed.

We rolled out localisation **section by section**, handing off each area to its owning team for testing and feedback before moving on.

### Keeping Everyone Aligned

**Constant communication** was absolutely vital here:
- Regularly liaising with teams to decide which areas we‚Äôd tackle next.  
- Checking when they were free to test ‚Äî translating a feature mid-refactor never ends well.  
- Sending **twice-weekly updates** to leadership to keep everyone aligned.

This was a real cross-team effort, and staying proactive with communication was the only reason it worked smoothly.

## Launch

We decided not to launch partially translated pages ‚Äî seeing half-English, half-Spanish content just didn‚Äôt feel right.

Instead, we rolled out as an **A/B test**, starting small and gradually ramping up to a 50/50 split over a few days.
